**The Notion structure:**
- **Clients DB:** Contact info, rates, contract dates, status
- **Projects DB:** Linked to clients, status, deadlines, deliverables
- **Invoices DB:** Linked to projects, auto-populated client data, payment status

**Why this matters:** One change in the Clients table propagates everywhere. Update a client's address? Every future invoice updates automatically.

## What Constraints Actually Do

There's this myth that you need *more* resources to build better products.

**Reality:** Constraints force clarity.

With 2 hours and 2.5 cents:
- I couldn't build a custom web app
- I couldn't integrate 6 different APIs
- I couldn't "just quickly" add one more feature

I had to ship the *smallest version* that solved the *real problem*.

**That's the entire product.** Notion template + invoices. That's it.

The moment I tried to add "just one more thing" ‚Äî a client portal, a time tracker, an automation ‚Äî was the moment I had to say: *"That's Product 2.0. This is Product 1.0."*
